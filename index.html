The Gemma 4 Model Family: Four Sizes, One Mission

Gemma 4 comes in four variants, each targeting a specific deployment scenario — from your phone to your workstation:

  • Gemma 4 E2B (Edge) — Ultra-lightweight for mobile and IoT devices. Native audio understanding. 128K context window. Dense architecture.
  • Gemma 4 E4B (Edge) — Balanced edge model with multimodal capabilities (text, image, video, audio). 128K context window. Dense architecture.
  • Gemma 4 26B MoE — Mixture-of-Experts model that activates only 4B parameters during inference. Runs on consumer GPUs. 256K context window.
  • Gemma 4 31B Dense — The flagship. Full dense architecture for maximum quality. Runs on a single workstation GPU. 256K context window.

The key insight in this lineup is the 26B MoE. While it has 26 billion total parameters, it only activates a 4B subset per token during inference. This means you get near-31B quality with inference speed closer to a 4B model — a massive efficiency gain for production deployments.

Multimodal by Design

Gemma 4 isn't just a text model with bolted-on vision. It's multimodal from the ground up, and the modality support varies intelligently by size:

  • All models: Text generation, image understanding (variable aspect ratio and resolution), video understanding, tool use, and reasoning
  • E2B and E4B only: Native audio understanding — enabling private, voice-activated AI agents on mobile devices without sending audio to the cloud

The video understanding capability is particularly noteworthy. Gemma 4 can analyze spatial relationships, interpret human interactions, and provide situational awareness from video input — capabilities that were previously the exclusive domain of much larger proprietary models.

This multimodal design directly targets the agentic AI paradigm. An agent built on Gemma 4 can see your screen (image), watch a process (video), listen to commands (audio on edge models), use tools, and reason through complex tasks — all locally on your hardware.

Benchmark Performance: Punching Way Above Its Weight

Google's claim that Gemma 4 "outcompetes models 20x its size" isn't marketing fluff. The numbers back it up:

  • GPQA Diamond (scientific reasoning): Gemma 4 31B scores 85.7% — the second highest ever recorded for an open model under 40B parameters, trailing only Qwen 3.5 27B at 85.8%
  • Arena AI Open Leaderboard: 31B Dense ranks #3 globally among all open models; 26B MoE ranks #6
  • Arena AI Overall: Highest-ranked Western open model — the top positions are dominated by Chinese open models (Qwen, DeepSeek, MiniMax)
  • Math (AIME) and Coding (LiveCodeBench): Massive leaps over Gemma 3, making Gemma 4 competitive with proprietary models like GPT-4 and Gemini Pro

Model Performance vs Size (Image credit: HuggingFace Gemma 4 Blog)

To put the 85.7% GPQA Diamond score in perspective: PhD experts achieve 65% accuracy on this benchmark, while skilled non-experts with web access only reach 34%. Gemma 4's 31B Dense is operating at a level that surpasses human domain experts.

The efficiency story is equally compelling. On GPQA Diamond, the entire top of the leaderboard is occupied by models in the 27-40B range — Gemma 4 31B, Qwen 3.5 27B, and a handful of others. The message is clear: the 30B parameter class has become the sweet spot for open-weight reasoning in 2026.

Apache 2.0: Truly Open

Gemma 4 is released under the Apache 2.0 license — the gold standard for open-source software. This means:

  • Commercial use — build products, ship to production, no revenue restrictions
  • Modification — fine-tune, distill, quantize, merge, whatever you need
  • Distribution — deploy on your own infrastructure, no API lock-in
  • No data sovereignty concerns — your data never leaves your hardware

This is a significant differentiator from the Gemma 1/2 era, which used a custom Gemma license with more restrictions. Apache 2.0 puts Gemma 4 on equal footing with Llama 4 and Qwen 3 in terms of openness.

The Gemma family has been downloaded over 400 million times since its initial release in February 2024. The Apache 2.0 license for Gemma 4 signals Google's long-term commitment to the open-weight ecosystem.

Deploy Anywhere: The Hardware Story

Google optimized Gemma 4 for the full spectrum of hardware. Here's where each model fits:

  • E2B / E4B: Smartphones, tablets, laptops, Raspberry Pi-class devices. Available through Google AI Edge Gallery. Uses MatFormer architecture with Per-Layer Embeddings (PLE) and parameter skipping for memory efficiency.
  • 26B MoE: Consumer GPUs (RTX 4090, RTX 5090), Apple Silicon (M-series Macs). Only 4B active parameters = fast inference on limited VRAM.
  • 31B Dense: Workstation GPUs (RTX 5090, A100 80GB, H100), Google Cloud TPUs. Available through Google AI Studio for testing without hardware.

The software ecosystem support is comprehensive:

  • Hugging Face Transformers — standard pipeline API with pipeline("image-text-to-text", model="google/gemma-4-31b-it")
  • llama.cpp — CPU/quantized inference for running on any hardware
  • MLX — Apple Silicon optimized inference (M1/M2/M3/M4 Macs)
  • ONNX / Transformers.js — run Gemma 4 directly in the browser via WebGPU. Hugging Face hosts a live demo.
  • Mistral.rs — Rust-based inference engine with UQFF quantization support
  • NVIDIA NIM and NeMo — enterprise deployment framework
  • Ollama — one-command local deployment
  • Docker — containerized deployment

Fine-Tuning: TRL and Unsloth Studio

One of the biggest advantages of open-weight models is fine-tuning. Gemma 4 supports two major fine-tuning paths:

TRL (Transformer Reinforcement Learning)

Hugging Face's TRL library works out of the box with Gemma 4. Standard supervised fine-tuning (SFT), DPO, and GRPO workflows are supported. You can fine-tune on a single GPU for the 26B MoE model.

Unsloth Studio

Unsloth provides 2-5x faster fine-tuning with dramatically reduced memory usage. Their custom CUDA kernels optimize the attention mechanism for Gemma 4's architecture. For the 26B MoE, Unsloth can enable fine-tuning on a single 24GB consumer GPU with 4-bit quantization.

# Fine-tune Gemma 4 26B with Unsloth
from unsloth import FastLanguageModel

model, tokenizer = FastLanguageModel.from_pretrained(
    model_name="google/gemma-4-26b-it",
    max_seq_length=4096,
    load_in_4bit=True,
)

model = FastLanguageModel.get_peft_model(
    model, r=16,
    target_modules=["q_proj", "k_proj", "v_proj", "o_proj"],
)

Gemma 4 vs. the 2026 Open Model Landscape

The open-source LLM space in April 2026 is more competitive than ever. Here's how Gemma 4 stacks up against the major players:

  • Gemma 4 31B — Best in class for multimodal reasoning at its size. Apache 2.0 license. Strongest Western open model. Excels at GPQA Diamond (85.7%) and scientific reasoning.
  • Qwen 3.5 27B — Narrowly edges out Gemma 4 on GPQA (85.8%) and leads on IFEval instruction following (92.6%). Chinese open model leader.
  • Llama 4 Maverick (671B total, 37B active MoE) — 10M token context window. Best for long-context tasks. Needs multi-GPU setup. More expensive to run.
  • DeepSeek V3.2 — Best overall open model by aggregate benchmarks. 671B/37B MoE. MIT license. But requires significant compute.
  • MiniMax M2.5 (196B) — S-tier on SWE-bench Verified (80.2%) for real-world coding. AIME 2025 score of 97.3.

The key takeaway: Gemma 4 31B occupies a unique niche. It's the model you choose when you need frontier-level reasoning on a single workstation GPU with full Apache 2.0 freedom. For teams that can't justify multi-GPU setups for Llama 4 or DeepSeek V3.2, Gemma 4 delivers 90%+ of the capability at a fraction of the hardware cost.

The Agentic Angle: Why Gemma 4 Matters for AI Agents

Gemma 4 is the first open model family explicitly built with agentic AI workflows in mind. Three capabilities make this possible:

  • Tool use: Gemma 4 can call functions, use APIs, and chain tools — the foundation of any agent framework
  • Multimodal understanding: Agents can see screenshots, watch processes, and (on edge models) listen to voice commands
  • Reasoning: 85.7% on GPQA Diamond means agents can handle complex, multi-step tasks that require deep understanding

The E2B and E4B edge models are particularly significant here. A voice-activated AI agent that runs entirely on your phone — no cloud API, no data leaving the device, no latency — is now a realistic possibility. Combined with Apache 2.0 licensing, this opens the door for commercial on-device AI assistants that were previously impossible with proprietary models.

How to Get Started

Try it in the cloud (no hardware needed)

Head to Google AI Studio (for 31B and 26B) or Google AI Edge Gallery (for E4B and E2B) to test Gemma 4 at full performance without any local setup.

Run locally with Hugging Face

from transformers import pipeline

pipe = pipeline(
    "image-text-to-text",
    model="google/gemma-4-31b-it",
    device="cuda",
    torch_dtype="auto"
)

messages = [{
    "role": "user",
    "content": [
        {"type": "text", "text": "What do you see in this image?"},
        {"type": "image", "url": "https://example.com/photo.jpg"}
    ]
}]

result = pipe(text=messages, max_new_tokens=500)
print(result[0]["generated_text"][-1]["content"])

Run locally with Ollama (one command)

ollama run gemma4:31b
